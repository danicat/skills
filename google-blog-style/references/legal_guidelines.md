# Legal Guidelines for AI Technical Writer Agent

This document outlines the legal and editorial guidelines for creating technical content. It consolidates guidance from public materials guidelines.

## 1. Product Claims and Representations
Ensure all claims are factual, supportable, and precise.

### 1.1 Accuracy and Substantiation
* **Truth in Advertising:** Do not make statements that are factually inaccurate, misleading, or exaggerated.
* **Avoid Superlatives:** Avoid "best," "simplest," "seamless," "frictionless," "effortless," "unmatched," or "#1" unless substantiated by approved metrics.
* **Avoid Absolutes:** Avoid "always," "never," "every," "all," "unlimited," "zero," "100%," or "guaranteed."
* **Round Down:** Round numbers down (e.g., "over 100 integrations" rather than "103 integrations").
* **Data Freshness:** Supporting data must be no more than 2 years old.

### 1.2 Security and Safety
* **Shared Responsibility:** Convey shared responsibility. Avoid implying Google takes full ownership.
* **Preferred Phrasing:** Use "helps secure," "more secure," "can provide security," or "helps protect."
* **No "Unbreakable" Claims:** Never describe products as "impenetrable," "bulletproof," or "ironclad."
* **Compliance:** Say "supports compliance," not "is compliant."
* **Incidents:** Do not disclose victim identities or unreleased vulnerabilities.

### 1.3 Availability and Pricing
* **Future Products:** Do not discuss future features or roadmaps unless authorized.
* **"Free" vs. "No Charge":** Use "Offered at no charge" or "at no cost" instead of "free" if there are associated costs.

### 1.4 AI and Machine Learning
* **Product Clarity:** Distinguish between "Gemini" (consumer) and Vertex AI products.
* **Training Data:** Do not imply Google trains models on customer data.

## 2. Competitive Comparisons
* **Be "Googley":** Focus on benefits, do not disparage competitors.
* **Fair Comparison:** Comparisons must be truthful, accurate, and supported by data.
* **Avoid "Lock-in" Language:** Avoid "locked in," "leverage" (as a verb), or "bundling."
* **Market Share:** Use "sectors" or "segments" instead of "market share."

## 3. Trademarks and Branding
* **Acronyms:** permitted ONLY for GCP, GKE, Cloud ML Engine. Use full name first.
* **Possessives:** Do not use product names as possessives (e.g., avoid "BigQuery's features").

## 4. Usage of Examples and Fictional Data
Use safe, standardized placeholders.

### 4.1 Domain Names and IP Addresses
* **Domains:** Use *only* `example.com`, `example.org`, or `example.net`.
* **IP Addresses:** Use Reserved Documentation blocks (`198.51.100.0/24`, `203.0.113.0/24`, `2001:db8::/32`).

### 4.2 Names and Entities
* **Companies:** Use `ExampleOrganization`, `EnterpriseExampleOrganization`, `StartupExampleOrganization`.
* **People:** Use "Alice and Bob" or generic names. No real people or fictional characters.

## 5. Third-Party Content
* **"Partner":** Only use if there is a signed Partnership Agreement. Otherwise use "collaborator."
* **Reuse:** Do not copy text/code without license verification.
* **Logos:** Avoid third-party logos unless essential.

## 6. Data Usage
* **Customer Data:** Requires Marketing Release. Remove PII.
* **Internal Metrics:** Do not use financial metrics not public in SEC filings.

## 7. Review Triggers
Flag for review if content:
* Mentions specific customers/partners/competitors.
* Makes comparative claims.
* Discusses Security, Privacy, or AI/ML predictions.
